History

Located in Kathonzweni, Makueni County, St Thomas Kavingoni Senior School has grown to become one of the most respected educational institutions in the region. With over 600 students, the school offers a rich environment of learning, discipline, and opportunity.

Our alumni network includes professionals, civil servants, entrepreneurs and community leaders who continue to make Makueni and Kenya proud.

School Life

Students enjoy a wide range of co-curricular activities including sports, drama, music, debate, and community service clubs. The school provides a safe, structured environment with academic support programs, remedial classes, and experienced teacher mentorship.

🔥 Breaking News

Principal Launches 3rd Edition of Form 4 Mathematics Kesha

By Mathematics Dept • May 2, 2026

Principal Mr. Nicholas Muange has officially launched the 3rd Edition of the Form 4 Mathematics Kesha programme, which kicked off yesterday with tremendous energy and a very high note. The launch was met with enthusiasm from both students and teachers, signalling the school's renewed commitment to improving Mathematics performance in the 2026 KCSE.

The programme brings together targeted revision, intensive practice with past KNEC papers, and one-on-one support for students who need it most. Mr. Muange challenged all Form 4 students to treat Mathematics as a priority subject this term. "Mathematics is the gateway to many opportunities — we will not leave any student behind," he said.

Note to Parents: Please encourage your Form 4 children to fully participate in this programme. It runs alongside regular classes and is designed to significantly boost their KCSE Mathematics grade.

Principal Launches 2026 Academic Excellence Drive

By Editorial Team • April 2026

Principal Mr. Nicholas Muange has launched the school's "Excellence 2026" initiative, aimed at improving the KCSE mean score from 6.5 to 7.0. The programme includes early morning revision sessions, enhanced teacher supervision, and regular student-teacher dialogue.

"Every student in this school has the potential to reach grade B and above. Our job as teachers is to unlock that potential," said Mr. Muange at the school assembly.

St Thomas Kavingoni Wins at Makueni County Music Festival

By Mr. Musila O.M • March 2026

The school's music ensemble clinched second place at the Makueni County Music Festival held in Wote. The choir performed a traditional Kamba folk song and a patriotic piece, earning praise from adjudicators for their rich harmonies and discipline.

The team will now represent Makueni County at the Eastern Regional Festival in April. The school wishes them all the best!

Creative Writing Corner

"Ndoto" — An Essay on Dreams and Education

By barrack Nyolo, Form 3 — Winner, School Essay Competition 2026

When I look at the hills of Makueni from our school grounds at dawn, I think about every student who has walked these paths. Education, I have come to believe, is not just about passing examinations. It is about discovering who you are — your strengths, your voice, your place in the world.

My teacher once told me: "The pen you hold today is more powerful than any tool your parents ever held." I carry those words with me into every examination room.
